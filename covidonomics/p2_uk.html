INFECTION FATALITY RATE BY AGE

What are the odds of dying if infected by Covid?


In England, the infection fatality rate (number of deaths /number of cases) is linked to the probability of dying if infected by the virus.
We represent this rate by age class :

  • In blue, the fatality rate computed over the last 7 days
  • In black, the fatality rate computed on all data

Conclusion: Case fatality rates in England are huge notably when compared with France.

  • Around 30% chance of dying if your over 80 and tested positive to the virus. In France, that rate is around 12%.
    • .
  • 70-74: 12 % chance
  • 65-69: 7 % chance
  • Under 60, the risk of dying is negligible

Why such a death rate in the UK? The discrepancy with France is resonating with a deeper mortality observed in the UK globally. Also to be considered : a difference in the testing policies making these death rates not comparable? Obesity? Vitamin D? Protection and healthcare to the elders?

LIFE EXPECTANCY OF COVID DEATHS

Here are the in-depth statistics of the Covid deaths, by age. Source is coronavirus.data.gov.uk.
Life expectancy comes from the Office for National Statistics (ONS).

  • Mean Life Expectancy of the Covid deaths
    • Mean age of the Covid deaths stands at 81.6 years. We also deduce the mean life expectancy of the deaths. The life expectancy of the Covid average 10.8 years.
    That estimate doesn't take into account that 96% of the Covid deaths had one or more pre-existing conditions (3.5 comorbidites on average based on Italian statistics, report here). The life expectancy accounting for pre-existing conditions is thus probably much lower than 10 years.
  • QALY value of the Covid deaths
    • In its 2013 reference document, NICE considers that interventions costing the authorities less than £20,000 per year of life saved (QALY) are cost effective. Applying this 20000£ value per year of life lost due to Covid of the 111 315 deaths with an average life expectancy of 10.8 years, these deaths are worth 24 B£.
    24 B£, the QALY valorisation of these 111k deaths following the NICE approach.
